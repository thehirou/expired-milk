Battle system

Hi programmer!

As much as I'd love to code this game 100% by myself there is always something about battle systems that I just can't program them, they are a nightmare for me, that's why I wrote this page! It should have almost everything you need to know about how the battle system should work, I'd love you to keep it commented because obviously I'll have to work over it after it's finished.

Basically everything should be happening in one room of the game, the only external thing in that room that is going to be used is probably going to be the textbox script. Your job is to make a flexible battle system where the attack patterns are easy to set up and custom. Make flags that let the player use certain skills if they are turned on. Camera movement.

Something missing? You don't understand something here? Just let me know directly.

Basic:

  • Enemy is on the right side of the screen, Apfel and Kase are on the left side.
  • By Apfel and Kase I mean Apfel with Kase because what is Kase going to do lol
  • You charge your attack by waiting and pressing the interact button (Z) at the right moment, then deliver the attack, if you press it too late you'll miss, if you press just in time frame perfect you'll make a critic attack, there could be power ups that increase that frame gap to make a perfect hit more often.
  • Levitating foes are not affected unless attacked with top alchemy (Air or Fire) or if you jump over them, using your bo staff as a jumping pole, the outcome of this will make the foe stay on the ground for 2 turns give or take. See image (2)
  • Bottom alchemy (Water or Earth) affects to foes that are on the ground. (Earth alchemy will never work because of lore that has to do with Mantana. The first time you try to use it it will be mentioned so you know that you can't never use it even though it's an option, I know, I know, it's frustrating...)
  • "Magic" is represented as alchemy in this battle system, you start knowing about fire, water and air alchemy (I skip earth because that one will not work until the final boss... Lore... yummy)
  • Risotto is the first battle, also first mini boss, when you fight him you won't be able to jump because you haven't unlocked that skill yet, after defeating Risotto you will obtain the jumping skill.
  • Parmesan is the second boss battle, you'll unlock some kind of skill like "Luck" since the battle against Parmesan is rock-paper-scissors oriented it makes sense. This skill might work like Thwack from the Dragon Quest series, there is a small chance to deal lethal damage to the foe when using this. By the way, in this battle you're not allowed to physically attack, only use the "Luck" skill.
  • The way foes attack you is the same as in Mario & Luigi, they attack you and the only way to protect yourself from these attacks is to jump over the ones that are jumpable, attack the ones that are attackable or counter the ones who can be countered. See image (5)
  • The backgrounds are 3d pre-rendered and parallaxed, the camera is centered on the middle of the battlefield but follows the action of whoever is attacking when that's the case.
  • To make this system more unique, it will be mixed with "Ni No Kuni" (DSi Edition) positioning battle system where the more away you are from the grid, your melee attacks won't do a lot of damage but your alchemy attacks will deal more damage, also your defense is boosted if you're on the back, if you're close to the enemy your melee damage is increased but you will receive more damage, if you stay in the middle it's like, neutral. See image (8)
  • You can be lucky and avoid some attacks by being in the correct spot of the grid, this way you can get more tactical once you find some kind of pattern the foe has. Some attacks are only avoidable doing this.
  • Somewhere I said that you only get skills after defeating bosses but you can also get alchemy skills if you find pages of the alchemy companion, Ni No Kuni style
  • There might be some fights where you can't attack at all and you have to try counter everything in order to win, also, most battles should allow you to do this in case you don't know how this system works.
  • If you lose a certain number of times you'll be granted a way to just skip over the battle if you want, before this happens, the difficulty will procedurally decrease without the player knowing.
  • The player always starts attacking, then the foe, unless you're stunned.
  • There are no battles when you're playing as Blume or Mantana.
  • There can be hazards in the battlefield that act as a different selectable foe but they don't attack, when attacking them it should result in something happening for the battle to progress: For example: an icicle hanging from the roof, you must attack it twice so it falls over an enemy that is being protected by some kind of wall. See image (7)
  • The battles are scripted, there will be no random encounters or enemies following you, there might be optional battles. I believe this is what Everhood does.
  • Some enemies can counter attacks, for example, some fights might need you to counter an attack countered by an enemy on repeat until the enemy fails, kinda like tennis. See image (6)
  • You start all battles with full health, after finishing the battle an animation of your health being replenished will play
  • Attacks are full 640x480 (/2) pixel art animations tied together so if you miss an attack the animation continues with you missing the attack and viceversa. Also the input press timing depends on the frame of the animation.
  • So to make the animations line up always with the foe and your position, after finishing selecting everything Apfel and the foe will line up to the center, no matter if Apfel's position was back, middle or front. See image (9)
  • Alchemy combos will always add up to the number of vertices the alchemical representation has so to make a full fire attack (Represented as a triangle) the player must perform a 3x combo, that is pressing the attack button on time thrice, you can just do 1x combo (For some attacks) but that will perform a weaker attack. The same for octagram (8 vertices) and for earth and air (Represented as a triangle with a line in the middle.) It will be four times. A graphic of how many out of the max combo you've got will be displayed on the background, or as a HUD element. See image (1)
  • To make a combo jumping you just have to hit the attack button just in time you are about to land after jumping for the first time, like in the Mario and Luigi RPG. The last attack being Apfel bonking the foe with her bo staff. See image (4)
  • To make a combo with alchemy you have to click attack on certain locations of a bar that moves from left to right. Those parts are represented by a different color from the background of the bar and the speed at which the bar moves and the different colors are supposed to be random. The animation is Apfel just standing still while you perform the combo. See image (3)
  • As I said somewhere, when you finish selecting your options and it's your turn to attack and your animation plays, Apfel and the foe will be centered to the middle so the animations line up, so very importantly, when you line up, a translucent grid will be set under Apfel's position so you can see where she was standing.
  • The foe can speak to you using textboxes (Already coded) before you start attacking or after they finish attacking, during this time you won't be able to start the attack until you close the textbox.
  • The UI should also be updated with the colors that the user selected.
  • There can be a max of two foes in a battle, usually there will be only one, still not sure if I want only one to attack or to make both attack in their turn.
  • When jump attacking a foe in the air, as previously mentioned you will only be able to attack them once, if the enemy selected is in the air, a different jumping animation will play for Apfel.
  • More info about Image (10): The timing patterns should be inside the animation, for example, if the correct timing to hit the foe is on the frame 24 of the animation, when the animation gets to frame 24 (+- the margin of error) the player has to press input to continue with the animation of hitting the foe or if missed, start playing the miss animation and going back animation. Maybe every timing can be inside a big animation in cases of multiple attack combo (alchemy, jumping...)
    Total of frames of a 100% success animation could, for example be 120 frames, well, the frames where the player has to press input could be, (1) 30+-margin, (2) 60+-margin and (3) 100+-margin, if the combo gets interrupted in the (2) second attack, the input listener would be disabled and it would start playing the miss animation.
  • Basically:
    Approach animation -> Init attack animation -> (Did the player press the input on time?)
    [Yes] -> Start playing attacking animation -> Deal damage -> Start playing going back animation
    [No] -> Start playing miss animation -> Start playing going back animation
    -> Foe's turn.

Skills:

  • Normal attack: Apfel dashes to the enemy, prepares her attack with the bo staff as if it were a hammer, then bonks the enemy, if she misses the bo staff will fall of her hands, if she deals critic damage she will "boing".
  • Jump attack: Unlocked after Risotto battle, this does less damage than a normal attack but it can stun floating foes, also, with "frame perfect" jumps you can deal up to three times more damage, last attack being a melee attack with the bo staff as Apfel lands from the last jump.
  • Counter attack: Attacks that are projectiles can be countered if you time the attack button just when they are about to hit you, some get redirected to the foe and some just save you from getting hit.
  • Jump defense: Some low-range attacks can be avoided if you jump over them.
  • Lucky attack: Unlocked after Parmesan battle, you'll play mouse, cheese, trap against the foe, if you win you deal an absurd amount of damage, if you lose you'll get an absurd amount of damage (It will never kill you, it will cap at 1 HP, unless you're already at 1 HP)
  • Air alchemy: Effective on taking flying foes to the ground but will not deal more than 1 of damage (Unless critic attack or full combo).
  • Fire alchemy: Write later.
  • Water alchemy: Write later.
  • Earth alchemy: You won't be able to use this alchemy until the last battle because Mantana is still alive, in stone form, but still alive, that's why the powers from Mantana are still kept inside them. Mantana represents earth, as a clarification. You will be able to use it, and you will have to use it to defeat Mantana in the final boss battle turning them into stone now that you have Mantana's ring.
  • Creation alchemy: Page from the alchemy companion, symbolized by the Octagram star, you can attack up to 8 times if you time everything correctly, if you miss more than 2 times you won't be able to attack in the next turn.

Basics of attack patterns:

  • I don't want to make every single attack a unique script because it's going to be hell to balance and modify, all the attacks will have a very basic operation that can be easily replaced with other graphics and damage, keep in mind, nothing of this is programmed so this is only concept patterns that I'm thinking about as I write.
  • Counterable / Counterable-return / Not counterable: Is a basic flag set to every attack pattern, counterable means that you can attack in the moment and avoid you from getting hit, counterable-return means that not only you can avoid getting hit but can return the projectile to the attacking foe, usually these two flags are set for attack patterns that are some kind of projectile (Most usually the return is for projectiles and counterable is for melee attacks), not magic, either way I'll try my best to make that if I set an attack to counterable no matter what you can counter it. Not counterable is an attack that you can jump over or you have to be lucky and avoid it by being in the correct position of the grid (back, middle, front)
    • The arguments could be (count_,count_return,count_not)
  • Projectiles: Attacks where the animation is go from point A to point B using an animation curve, usually point A being the attacker, and point B being the receiver.
    • The arguments could be (pointsummon, point_arrival, animation_curve)[count/count_return/count_not]
  • Melee: This one will need to trigger the full 640x480 animation and the combo is dependent on the input depending on which frame you pressed the attack button.
    • The arguments could be (animation_file,[combo in frame 1, 2, 10, 30, XX, YY, ZZ])
